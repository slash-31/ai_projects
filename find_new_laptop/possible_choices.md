## Critical Purchasing Guidance

### Before You Buy - Essential Verification Steps:

1. **Verify Cellular Connectivity is Included**
   - LTE/5G is almost always an optional upgrade
   - Check the spec sheet for "WWAN" or "Mobile Broadband"
   - Look for specific modem chipset (Qualcomm X55/X65, Intel XMM series)
   - Business/enterprise models have it; consumer models typically don't

2. **Confirm Physical SIM or eSIM Support**
   - Most modern business laptops use eSIM
   - Verify your carrier supports eSIM activation for laptops
   - Some models have both physical nano-SIM and eSIM

3. **Check Carrier Compatibility**
   - Verify the modem supports your carrier's bands
   - 5G sub-6 is widely compatible in US
   - Cat 16 LTE is excellent fallback

4. **Verify the Exact Model/SKU**
   - Manufacturers use different SKUs for WWAN vs non-WWAN
   - Example: ThinkPad X1 Carbon Gen 11 has dozens of SKUs
   - WWAN models often have specific part numbers

5. **Consider Buying Direct from Manufacturer**
   - Dell, Lenovo, and HP allow custom configuration
   - Retail models (Best Buy, Amazon) often lack WWAN option
   - Business/enterprise sales channels have better WWAN availability

## The 15" Screen Reality Check

Both Claude and Gemini identified the same market reality: **15" laptops with built-in cellular connectivity are essentially unavailable in the current market.**

**Why this limitation exists:**
- Cellular connectivity is a business/enterprise feature
- Business users prioritize portability (13-14" form factor)
- 15" laptops target consumers/home users who use WiFi
- Mobile professionals want lightweight devices for travel
- Manufacturers segment their product lines accordingly

**Your Options:**

**Option A: Accept 13.5-14" Display**
This is the recommended path. Modern 14" displays with high resolution (2.8K, QHD+) provide excellent screen real estate, often comparable to older 15" displays due to higher pixel density and better aspect ratios.

**Option B: External Display for Desk Work**
Use a 13.5-14" laptop with cellular for mobility, then connect to an external monitor when at desk. This is how many professionals work.

**Option C: Portable WiFi Hotspot**
Buy a 15" laptop without cellular (like Surface Laptop 5 15") and use a separate mobile hotspot device. This adds complexity but gives you the 15" screen.

**Option D: Wait for Market Changes**
The laptop market evolves slowly. A 15" business laptop with 5G may eventually appear, but there's no indication this is coming in 2025.

**Recommendation**: Choose Option A (14" with cellular). The productivity gain from built-in cellular far outweighs the 1" screen reduction, especially with modern high-resolution displays.
